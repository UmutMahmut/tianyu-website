天语计划项目介绍

图：天语-I 望远镜渲染图

项目背景

天语计划（Tianyu Project，简称 JUST-P）是由上海交通大学发起和主导的天文观测项目， 致力于探索时间域天文学与系外行星研究。项目名称源于李政道先生提出的“以天为语”， 寓意借助天文语言洞察宇宙规律。JUST-P 是 JUST 项目的子计划，现已独立发展并形成专属科研方向。

项目目标

项目拟建设一台具备高时间分辨率和大视场能力的光学望远镜（天语-I）， 实施长期持续的巡天观测。目标星数预计超过一千万，重点覆盖 Kepler/TESS 的连续观测区（CVZ）， 用于捕捉光变事件、凌星现象及多种时间域天体活动。

技术参数

  • 口径：1米
  • 视场：10平方度
  • 覆盖星数：约1000万颗
  • 采样频率：最高每小时一次
  • 重点区域：Kepler/TESS 连续观测区（CVZ）

科学方向

天语计划支持分钟至月级的多时间尺度观测模式，适配多样化科学需求。主要研究方向包括但不限于：

  • 系外行星：高精度光变监测，寻找长周期、低信噪比的凌星事件
  • 变星与爆发源：周期性与随机性变化的系统研究
  • 近地天体：跟踪小行星轨迹，辅助预警
  • TNO 掩星：观测海王星外天体的掩星事件
  • 早期超新星：快速识别与跟踪初期爆发现象
  • 快速蓝光瞬变（FBOT）：探测稀有快光变事件
  • 多信使配对：搜索伽马射线暴（GRB）和快速射电暴（FRB）的光学对应体

项目意义

天语计划将补充我国在高时域分辨率巡天方面的能力，为系外行星和瞬变天体研究提供宝贵的数据资源。 项目也将积累未来更大型时间域设施所需的运行和处理经验。

国际合作与开放

项目采用开放合作机制，欢迎全球科研人员参与数据分析与科学研究。 后续将搭建规范化的数据访问平台，逐步提供数据下载和接口服务。

Tianyu Project Overview

Fig: Tianyu-I Telescope Rendering

Background

The Tianyu Project (JUST-P) is a dedicated time-domain astronomy initiative led by Shanghai Jiao Tong University. Inspired by Nobel Laureate Tsung-Dao Lee’s idea of “understanding nature through the language of the sky,” Tianyu focuses on exoplanet discovery and optical transient science. Originally a sub-project of JUST, it now follows an independent scientific roadmap.

Goals

The project aims to build a high-cadence, wide-field optical telescope (Tianyu-I) capable of long-term survey monitoring. It will observe over 10 million stars, concentrating on the continuous viewing zone (CVZ) of Kepler/TESS to detect transits, brightness variations, and other dynamic phenomena.

Technical Parameters

  • Aperture: 1 meter
  • Field of View: 10 deg²
  • Target Star Count: ~10 million
  • Cadence: up to once per hour
  • Focus Region: Kepler/TESS CVZ

Science Topics

Tianyu supports multi-scale temporal monitoring (minutes to months) to meet diverse research goals. Scientific directions include:

  • Exoplanets: Detecting long-period, low-SNR transits
  • Variables and Outbursts: Periodic and stochastic light curve variations
  • NEOs: Tracking near-Earth asteroid paths
  • TNO Occultations: Monitoring distant solar system bodies
  • Early Supernovae: Identifying initial outbursts and triggering follow-up
  • FBOTs: Capturing fast blue optical transients
  • Multi-Messenger Events: Searching for optical counterparts to GRBs and FRBs

Significance

Tianyu fills a crucial gap in China's time-domain survey capabilities, offering high-precision, long-baseline photometric data. It will also provide valuable operational and data processing experience for future large-scale observatories.

Open Collaboration

The project follows an open and collaborative model, encouraging global participation in data analysis and scientific discovery. A dedicated data platform is under development to support access and integration.
