Located in Lenghu Town, Mangya City, Qinghai Province at an elevation of ~4,200 meters, the Lenghu astronomical site has been identified as a premier high-altitude location in western China. Several facilities are already under construction, and the Tianyu telescope is planned to be stationed here. The region offers a dry climate, low atmospheric turbulence, and favorable seeing conditions— making it well-suited for high-precision time-domain observations.

Advantages of High Altitude Observing

  • Reduced Air Mass: 4,200m altitude decreases scattering and absorption
  • Low Water Vapor: PWV often below 2 mm during nighttime
  • Excellent Seeing: Median seeing ~0.75 arcsec (2021 data)
  • High Clear Night Ratio: Over 270 clear nights annually

Astronomical Suitability

Evaluated to offer internationally competitive conditions, Lenghu is ideal for long-term, high-precision time-domain monitoring projects targeting exoplanets, transients, and variable stars.
